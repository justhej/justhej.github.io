Swp_1.3: Loop Dogg

2023-12-30
1 minute read

The final puzzle in chapter 3 is:

Called “Four stash sweep”. Playgrounds expects the user to write something like a function that is looped over, like so:

 1func pattern(){
 2  moveForward()
 3  collectGem()
 4  moveForward()
 5  collectGem()
 6  turnLeft()
 7  turnLeft()
 8  moveForward()
 9  moveForward()
10  turnRight()
11  moveForward()
12  collectGem()
13  turnLeft()
14  turnLeft()
15  moveForward()
16  moveForward()
17  collectGem()
18  moveForward()
19}
20
21for _ in 1...4{
22  pattern()
23}

However, I think it’s best to teach good practices from the get-go. So I fired up a tentative solution that was less bloaty:

 1func turnAround(){
 2    turnLeft(); turnLeft()
 3}
 4
 5
 6func pattern(){
 7    var steps = 0
 8    while steps < 7{
 9        moveForward()
10        steps += 1
11        if isOnGem{
12            collectGem()
13        }
14        if steps == 3{
15            turnRight()
16        }
17        if steps == 4 || steps == 2 {
18            turnAround()
19        }
20    }
21}
22
23for _ in 1...4{
24    pattern()
25}

And refactored it to this final form:

 1func patternB() {
 2    for k in 1...7 {
 3        moveForward()
 4        collectGem()
 5        if k == 2 || k == 4 {
 6            turnLeft(); turnLeft()
 7        } else if k == 3 {
 8            turnRight()
 9        }
10    }
11}
12
13for _ in 1...4 {
14    patternB()
15}

This was the last puzzle. There are a total of six in chapter 3, and for some I wrote more than two solutions. In total, there are 280 lines of code.
